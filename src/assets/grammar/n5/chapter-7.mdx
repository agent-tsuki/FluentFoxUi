## Concept 01: Means & Tools (N [tool/means] で V)
We’ve used **で (de)** for transportation and locations. Now, we use it to indicate the **instrument or method** used to complete an action.

* **はしで食（た）べます。**
    * *Hashi de tabemasu.*
    * I eat with chopsticks.
* **日本語（にほんご）でレポートを書（か）きます。**
    * *Nihongo de repōto o kakimasu.*
    * I will write the report in Japanese.

---

## Concept 02: Foreign Language Equivalent
To ask how to say something in another language, use the pattern: **「Word/Sentence」は [Language] で 何（なん）ですか。**

* **「Thank you」は日本語で何ですか。**
    * *"Thank you" wa Nihongo de nan desu ka.*
    * What is "Thank you" in Japanese?
    * **...「ありがとう」です。** (...It’s "Arigatou".)

---

## Concept 03: Giving (あげます)
The particle **に (ni)** marks the **recipient** (the person receiving the item).

> **Pattern:** [Giver] は [Recipient] **に** [Object] を あげます。

* **[わたしは] 木村（きむら）さんに花（はな）をあげました。**
    * *Watashi wa Kimura-san ni hana o agemashita.*
    * I gave some flowers to Ms. Kimura.
* **イーさんに本（ほん）を貸（か）しました。**
    * *Ī-san ni hon o kashimashita.*
    * I lent a book to Ms. Lee.

---

## Concept 04: Receiving (もらいます)
The particle **に (ni)** marks the **source** (the person you received from). You can also use **から (kara)**, especially when receiving from an organization like a school or company.

> **Pattern:** [Receiver] は [Source] **に/から** [Object] を もらいます。

* **[わたしは] 山田（やまだ）さんに花（はな）をもらいました。**
    * *Watashi wa Yamada-san ni hana o moraimashita.*
    * I received flowers from Mr. Yamada.
* **銀行（ぎんこう）からお金（かね）を借（か）りました。**
    * *Ginkou kara okane o karimashita.*
    * I borrowed money from the bank.

---

## Concept 05: Already vs. Not Yet (もう / まだ)
* **もう (Mou)**: Already. Used with the past tense (**〜ました**).
* **まだ (Mada)**: Not yet. Used with **〜ていません** (Lesson 31) or simply **まだです**.

### Example: The Baggage Question
* **もう荷物（にもつ）を送（おく）りましたか。**
    * *Mou nimotsu o okurimashita ka.*
    * Have you sent the baggage yet?
    * **...はい、[もう] 送りました。** (Yes, I have [already] sent it.)
    * **...いいえ、まだです。** (No, not yet.)

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### Omission of Particles
In informal speech, particles like **は**, **が**, or **を** are often omitted if the context is obvious.
* *Formal:* このスプーンは、すてきですね。
* *Informal:* **このスプーン、すてきですね。** (This spoon is lovely, isn't it?)

### Pro-Tip: The "Ni" vs "Kara" Distinction
While you can use **に** for people you receive things from, you **must** use **から** when the sender is an institution.
* *Correct:* 学校 **から** もらいました。(Received from school)
* *Awkward:* 学校 **に** もらいました。
