## Concept 01: Pre-action vs. Post-action (とき)
We revisit **とき** to clarify exactly when an action occurs in relation to the main verb. The tense of the verb before `toki` changes the meaning entirely.

### 1. [V-Dictionary Form] とき
The action in the main clause happens **before** or **during** the action in the `toki` clause.
* **日本へ 行く とき、かばんを 買いました。**
    * *Nihon e iku toki, kaban o kaimashita.*
    * I bought a bag **on the way to** Japan (I didn't have the bag yet when I started).

### 2. [V-た Form] とき
The action in the main clause happens **after** the action in the `toki` clause is completed.
* **日本へ 行った とき、かばんを 買いました。**
    * *Nihon e itta toki, kaban o kaimashita.*
    * I bought a bag **after I arrived** in Japan.

## Concept 02: Noun Phrases for Specific States (〜という)
This pattern is used to define or name a specific noun. It literally means "The [Noun] called '[Name/Phrase]'."

> **Pattern:** [Sentence/Name] という [Noun]

* **「さくら」という 店（みせ）を 知（し）っていますか。**
    * *'Sakura' to iu mise o shitte imasu ka.*
    * Do you know the shop called "Sakura"?

---

## Concept 03: Explaining Reasons (〜ので)
Similar to `〜から`, **〜ので (node)** is used to state a reason. However, `〜ので` is more polite and objective, making it ideal for making excuses or asking for permission.

| Word Type | Connection | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Verb / い-adj** | Plain Form + ので | 行くので / 暑いので |
| **な-adj / Noun** | Add **な** + ので | 暇（ひま）な ので / 雨（あめ）な ので |

* **用事（ようじ）が あるので、お先（さき）に 失礼（しつれい）します。**
    * *Youji ga aru node, osaki ni shitsurei shimasu.*
    * Because I have errands, I will leave now (Polite).

---

## Concept 04: Interrogative + Verb + たら いいですか
As a final review of the `tara` form, this chapter emphasizes using it for specific instructions.

* **どこで カメラを 買（か）ったら いいですか。**
    * *Doko de kamera o kattara ii desu ka.*
    * Where should I buy a camera?
* **...秋葉原（あきはばら）へ 行ったら いいですよ。**
    * (You should go to Akihabara.)

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### 〜ので vs. 〜から
* **〜から:** Subjective. Focuses on the speaker's reasoning or opinion. (e.g., "Because *I* want to, I'll go.")
* **〜ので:** Objective. Often used for social reasons, weather, or circumstances beyond one's control.


### Cultural Insight: The "O-saki ni" Culture
When leaving work before others (perhaps because you have `youji ga aru node`), it is mandatory to say **お先に失礼します** (Osaki ni shitsurei shimasu). This chapter teaches you the grammar to explain *why* you are leaving in a way that remains professional.
