## Concept 01: Place Demonstratives (ここ・そこ・あそこ)
These words indicate location. Just like the objects in Lesson 2, these depend on the relative distance between the speaker and the listener.

> **Spatial Context (Ko-So-A-Do System):**
> * **ここ** (Koko) → The place where the **speaker** is.
> * **そこ** (Soko) → The place where the **listener** is.
> * **あそこ** (Asoko) → A place distant from **both**.

### Politeness Level: The Directional Series
**こちら, そちら, あちら** (Kochira, Sochira, Achira) refer to directions, but they are also used as the **polite alternatives** to Koko, Soko, and Asoko. Use these when speaking to a superior or a customer.

## Concept 02: Locating People & Things (N は Place です)
This pattern is used to state where a person, thing, or room is located.

    * *Otearai wa asoko desu.*
    * The restroom is over there.
* **<InteractiveWord kanji="電話" reading="でんわ" meaning="Telephone" /> は <InteractiveWord kanji="2階" reading="にかい" meaning="Second Floor" /> です。**
    * *Denwa wa ni-kai desu.*

* **山田さんは <InteractiveWord kanji="事務所" reading="じむしょ" meaning="Office" /> です。**
    * *Yamada-san wa jimusho desu.*
    * Mr. Yamada is in the office.

## Concept 03: Asking "Where?" (どこ・どちら)
* **どこ (Doko)**: Where?
* **どちら (Dochira)**: Which direction? / Where? (Polite)

> **Pro Tip:** When asking about a person's school, company, or country, Japanese uses **どこ** or **どちら** instead of "What."
> * **Correct:** 学校はどこですか。 (Where/What school do you go to?)
> * **Incorrect:** 学校は何ですか。

### Identifying Origins
* **<InteractiveWord kanji="お国" reading="おくに" meaning="Country" /> はどちらですか。**
    * *O-kuni wa dochira desu ka.*
    * Which country are you from? (Very polite)

## Concept 04: Origin & Manufacture ($N_1$ の $N_2$)
In this lesson, the particle **の** links a place/company ($N_1$) to a product ($N_2$).

### Example Sentences
* **これは <InteractiveWord kanji="日本" reading="にほん" meaning="Japan" /> のコンピューターです。**
    * *Kore wa Nihon no konpyūta desu.*
    * This is a Japanese computer (Made in Japan).
* **これはパワー <InteractiveWord kanji="電気" reading="でんき" meaning="Electricity" /> のコンピューターです。**
    * *Kore wa Pawā-denki no konpyūta desu.*
    * This is a Power Electric computer (Made by Power Electric).

## Concept 05: The Master Ko-So-A-Do Table
This table summarizes everything learned in Lessons 2 and 3.

| Category | KO (Near Speaker) | SO (Near Listener) | A (Distant Both) | DO (Question) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Things** | これ | それ | あれ | どれ |
| **Thing + N** | この N | その N | あの N | どの N |
| **Places** | ここ | そこ | あそこ | どこ |
| **Direction** | こちら | そちら | あちら | どちら |


## Sidebar: Reference & Culture

### Territories of Speech
In Japanese, if the speaker regards the listener as being in their "same territory" (e.g., standing right next to each other), the immediate area becomes **ここ**. The area slightly away becomes **そこ**, and far away is **あそこ**.

### Politeness Booster: The Prefix お-
The prefix **お-** is added to show respect to the listener or to make the word sound more refined/elegant.
* `くに` (country) becomes → `おくに`
* `てあらい` (restroom) becomes → `おてあらい`
