## Concept 01: Expressing Desire for an Object (N が ほしいです)
When you want a physical thing or a person (like a friend or child), use the adjective **ほしい (hoshii)**.

> **Key Rule:** The object of your desire is marked with the particle **が**.

* **[わたしは] 新（あたら）しい 車（くるま）が ほしいです。**
    * *Watashi wa atarashii kuruma ga hoshii desu.*
    * I want a new car.
* **若（わか）い 友達（ともだち）が ほしいです。**
    * *Wakai tomodachi ga hoshii desu.*
    * I want young friends.

## Concept 02: Expressing Desire to do an Action (V-tai)
To say you "want to do" something, you replace the **ます** of a verb with **たい**. This makes the verb behave like an **い-adjective**.

### Conjugation Table (V-tai)
| Form | Affirmative | Negative |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Present** | 〜たいです | 〜たくないです |
| **Past** | 〜たかったです | 〜たくなかったです |

* **日本（にほん）へ 行（い）きたいです。** (I want to go to Japan.)
* **何（なに）も 食（た）べたくないです。** (I don't want to eat anything.)

> **Particle Note:** With **〜たい**, you can use either **を** or **が** to mark the object.
> * ジュース **を** 飲みたいです。 (Normal)
> * ジュース **が** 飲みたいです。 (Slightly more emphasis on the juice)

## Concept 03: Purpose of Movement (Place へ Purpose に 行きます)
This is a very common pattern used to explain *why* you are going somewhere. The purpose can be a **Noun** or a **Verb Stem**.

> **Pattern:** Place へ [V-stem / N] **に** 行きます/来ます/帰ります。

* **デパートへ 買（か）い物（もの）に 行（い）きます。**
    * *Depāto e kaimono ni ikimasu.*
    * I am going to the department store **for** shopping. (Noun)
* **日本へ 日本語（にほんご）を 勉（べん）強（きょう）しに 来（き）ました。**
    * *Nihon e Nihongo o benkyou shi ni kimashita.*
    * I came to Japan **to study** Japanese. (Verb Stem)

---

## Concept 04: The Particle で (Total Number/Price)
The particle **で** is used here to indicate a limit or a total quantity.

* **この ケーキは 3つで 500円（ごひゃくえん）です。**
    * *Kono kēki wa mittsu de go-hyaku en desu.*
    * These cakes are 500 yen **for** three.

---

## Concept 05: Asking for Suggestions (どこか / なにか)
You can add **か** to question words to create indefinite pronouns.
* **どこか** (Somewhere / Anywhere)
* **なにか** (Something / Anything)

* **冬休（ふゆやす）みは どこか 行（い）きましたか。**
    * *Fuyuyasumi wa dokoka ikimashita ka.*
    * Did you go **anywhere** during winter break?
    * **...はい、北海道（ほっかいどう）へ 行きました。** (...Yes, I went to Hokkaido.)

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### Important Constraint: Desiring for Others
You cannot use **ほしい** or **〜たい** to describe what someone else wants. These are strictly for the speaker's own feelings (1st person). To talk about others, Japanese uses different forms like *garu* or *sou*, which you will learn later.

### Cultural Insight: Inviting vs. Wanting
Be careful using **〜たいですか** when inviting a superior. Asking "Do you want to eat?" (*Tabetai desu ka?*) can sound a bit too direct or rude. It is better to use the **〜ませんか** form from Chapter 6.
