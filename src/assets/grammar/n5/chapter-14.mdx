## Concept 01: Verb Groups
Before learning the て-form, you must know that Japanese verbs fall into three groups based on how they conjugate.

1.  **Group I:** Verbs ending in sounds like *i, chi, ri, mi, bi, ni, ki, gi, shi* before ~masu.
2.  **Group II:** Verbs ending in an *e* sound before ~masu (e.g., *tabemasu*), plus a few exceptions (e.g., *mimasu*, *okimasu*).
3.  **Group III:** Irregular verbs — **します** (to do) and **きます** (to come).

---

## Concept 02: Conjugating the て-form (Te-form)
The conjugation for Group I is the most complex. Use this "cheat sheet" logic for your UI:

### Group I Conjugation Rules:
| ~masu ending | Changes to... | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **い, ち, り** | 〜って (tte) | 買います → **買って** |
| **み, び, に** | 〜んで (nde) | 飲みます → **飲んで** |
| **き** | 〜いて (ite) | 書きます → **書いて** |
| **ぎ** | 〜いで (ide) | 急ぎます → **急いで** |
| **し** | 〜して (shite) | 貸します → **貸して** |
| *Exception* | 行きます | 行きます → **行って** |

### Group II & III (Easy):
* **Group II:** Just replace **ます** with **て** (e.g., 食べます → 食べた).
* **Group III:** します → **して** / きます → **きて**.

## Concept 03: Making Polite Requests (〜てください)
Use this pattern to ask someone to do something politely.

* **ちょっと 待（ま）ってください。**
    * *Chotto matte kudasai.*
    * Please wait a moment.
* **ここにお名前（なまえ）を 書（か）いてください。**
    * *Koko ni onamae o kaite kudasai.*
    * Please write your name here.

---

## Concept 04: Actions in Progress (〜ています)
This expresses that an action is currently happening (the "Present Continuous" in English).

* **ミラーさんは 今（いま） 電話（でんわ）を かけています。**
    * *Mirā-san wa ima denwa o kakete imasu.*
    * Mr. Miller is making a phone call right now.
* **今（いま） 雨（あめ）が 降（ふ）っていますか。**
    * *Ima ame ga futte imasu ka.*
    * Is it raining now?

---

## Concept 05: Offering Help (〜ましょうか)
Use this when you see someone in trouble and want to offer your assistance.

* **荷物（にもつ）を 持（も）ちましょうか。**
    * *Nimotsu o mochimashou ka.*
    * Shall I carry your luggage?
    * **...すみません。お願（ねが）いします。** (...Thank you. Please do.)

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### Directional Particle Note: が
When describing natural phenomena like rain or snow falling, the subject is marked with **が** instead of **は**.
* **雨（あめ）が 降（ふ）っています。** (It is raining.)


### Cultural Insight: The Softness of 'Kudasai'
While `~te kudasai` is polite, it is still a request/command. When asking for a big favor from a superior, Japanese speakers often use even more indirect forms which you will learn in higher levels. For daily classroom or office use, this form is perfect!
