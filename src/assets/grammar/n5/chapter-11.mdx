## Concept 01: How to Count (General Objects)
When counting objects that don't fall into a specific category, use the **一つ (hitotsu)** series. These are used up to 10. For numbers above 10, use the standard cardinal numbers.

| Number | Japanese | Reading |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | 一つ | ひとつ (Hitotsu) |
| 2 | 二つ | ふたつ (Futatsu) |
| 3 | 三つ | みつ (Mitsu) |
| 4 | 四つ | よつ (Yotsu) |
| 5 | 五つ | いつつ (Itsutsu) |

> **Key Rule:** Quantifiers (like *hitotsu* or *sannin*) are usually placed **after** the particle and **before** the verb.
> * **りんごを 四つ（よつ） 買（か）いました。** (I bought four apples.)

---

## Concept 02: Specialized Counters
Japanese uses specific suffixes based on the type of object.



1.  **〜人 (nin):** People. (Note: 1 person = *hitori*, 2 people = *futari*).
2.  **〜台 (dai):** Machines, cars, and electronic devices.
3.  **〜枚 (mai):** Flat, thin objects (paper, stamps, shirts).
4.  **〜回 (kai):** Number of times (frequency).

---

## Concept 03: Time Periods & Durations
To express how long an action lasts, we add **間 (kan)** to time units.

* **時間（じかん）**: Hours (e.g., *yo-jikan* = 4 hours).
* **週間（しゅうかん）**: Weeks (e.g., *is-shuukan* = 1 week).
* **か月（かげつ）**: Months (e.g., *ni-kagetsu* = 2 months).
* **年（ねん）**: Years (e.g., *san-nen* = 3 years).

### Particle Usage for Frequency
Use **に** to show the frequency within a period: **[Period] に [Frequency] V**.
* **1か月（いっかげつ）に 2回（にかい） 映画（えいが）を 見（み）ます。**
    * *Ikka-getsu ni ni-kai eiga o mimasu.*
    * I watch movies twice a month.

---

## Concept 04: "Only" (だけ vs しか)
Both express a limit, but they are used differently.

1.  **だけ (dake):** "Only/Just." Used with affirmative sentences.
    * **休みは 日曜日（どようび）だけです。** (Holiday is only Sunday.)
2.  **しか (shika):** "Only/Nothing but." Used **always** with negative sentences to show a sense of "not enough."
    * **休みは 日曜日しか ありません。** (There is no holiday but Sunday.)

---

## Concept 05: Approximate Quantities (ぐらい)
Add **ぐらい (gurai)** after a quantifier to mean "about" or "approximately."

* **30分（さんじゅっぷん）ぐらい 散歩（さんぽ）しました。**
    * *San-jup-pun gurai sanpo shimashita.*
    * I went for a walk for about 30 minutes.

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### Interrogative: How many?
* **いくつ**: General objects.
* **何人（なんにん）**: How many people?
* **何枚（なんまい）**: How many flat objects?
* **どのくらい**: How long? (duration).

### Cultural Insight: The Hitori/Futari Exception
Japanese people take great care in counting people. Always remember that 1 and 2 are irregular. Using "ichi-nin" or "ni-nin" will sound very unnatural to a native speaker!

| 近い | Chikai | Near |
