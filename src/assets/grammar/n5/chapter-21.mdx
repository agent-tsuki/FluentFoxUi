## Concept 01: Time (〜とき)
Use **とき (toki)** to connect two sentences when the first sentence indicates the time or circumstance when the second action occurs.

> **Pattern:** [Plain Form Clause] とき、[Main Sentence]。

### Conjugations with とき:
| Word Type | Connection | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Verb** | Plain Form | 寝（ね）る **とき** (When I sleep) |
| **い-adj** | Keep 〜い | 忙（いそが）しい **とき** (When I'm busy) |
| **な-adj** | Add **な** | 暇（ひま）**な** **とき** (When I'm free) |
| **Noun** | Add **の** | 病気（びょうき）**の** **とき** (When I'm sick) |

* **図書館（としょかん）で 本（ほん）を 借（か）りる とき、カードが 要（い）ります。**
    * *Toshokan de hon o kariru toki, kādo ga irimasu.*
    * When you borrow books at the library, you need a card.

---

## Concept 02: Tense in "Toki" Clauses
The tense of the verb before **とき** depends on whether the action is completed *before* or *after* the main action.

1.  **Dictionary Form + とき:** Action is not yet finished.
    * **国（くに）へ 帰（かえ）る とき、かばんを 買（か）いました。**
    * (I bought a bag **on the way back** to my country.)
2.  **た-form + とき:** Action is finished.
    * **国へ 帰（かえ）った とき、かばんを買いました。**
    * (I bought a bag **after I arrived back** in my country.)

---

## Concept 03: Natural Consequences (〜と)
The particle **と (to)** is used for "if" or "when" in situations where the result is a **natural consequence**, a mechanical reaction, or a fixed truth.

> **Pattern:** [V Dictionary Form] と、[Result]。

* **この ボタンを 押（お）す と、お釣（つ）りが 出（で）ます。**
    * *Kono botan o osu to, otsuri ga demasu.*
    * If you press this button, the change comes out.
* **これを聞（き）く と、元気（げんき）に なります。**
    * (When I hear this, I feel energized.)



---

## Concept 04: Indicating Directions (Noun を Position へ V)
When giving directions (like "turn right at the corner"), use the particle **を** for the path and **へ** for the direction.

* **交差点（こうさてん）を 右（みぎ）へ 曲（ま）がって ください。**
    * *Kousaten o migi e magatte kudasai.*
    * Please turn right at the intersection.

---

## Concept 05: The Subject Particle が (ga) in Time Clauses
Just like in Chapter 20, if the "Toki" clause has its own subject, it must be marked with **が**.

* **友達（ともだち）が 来（く）る とき、ご飯（ごはん）を 作（つく）ります。**
    * *Tomodachi ga kuru toki, gohan o tsukurimasu.*
    * When my friend comes over, I cook a meal.

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### Important Constraint: 'To' (Natural Result)
You **cannot** use **〜と** if the second half of the sentence is a request, a command, or an invitation.
* *Wrong:* 時間（じかん）が ある と、手伝（てつだ）って ください。
* *Correct:* 時間が ある **とき**、手伝ってください。
