## Concept 01: Telling Time (〜時 〜分)
To express time, we use the counters **時 (ji)** for hours and **分 (fun/pun)** for minutes. 

> **Key Rule:** Minutes can be tricky! Use **-fun** after 2, 5, 7, 9 and **-pun** after 1, 3, 4, 6, 8, 10.
> * **1:05** → 一時五分 (Ichi-ji go-fun)
> * **1:10** → 一時十分 (Ichi-ji jup-pun)
> * **Half past ($:30$)** → 〜時半 (ji han)

### Example Sentences
* **今何時（いまなんじ）ですか。**
    * *Ima nan-ji desu ka.*
    * What time is it now?
* **七時十分（しちじじゅっぷん）です。**
    * *7-ji 10-pun desu.*
    * It is 7:10.

## Concept 02: Polite Verb Conjugations (〜ます)
Verbs in the **ます (masu)** form show politeness to the listener. This form is used for habitual actions or future events.

### The Conjugation Table
| Tense | Affirmative (Yes) | Negative (No) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Non-Past** | 〜ます (masu) | 〜ません (masen) |
| **Past** | 〜ました (mashita) | 〜ませんでした (masendeshita) |

### Usage Examples
* **毎朝（まいあさ）6時に起（お）きます。** (I get up at 6 every morning.)
* **昨日（きのう）勉強（べんきょう）しましたか。** (Did you study yesterday?)
    * **...はい、勉強しました。** (Yes, I did.)
    * **...いいえ、勉強しませんでした。** (No, I didn't.)

## Concept 03: The Time Particle (に)
The particle **に (ni)** is used to mark the specific point in time when an action occurs.

> **The "Ni" Filter:**
> 1. **Required:** Numbers and Days of the week (e.g., *6-ji ni*, *Getsuyoubi ni*).
> 2. **Optional:** Time periods like Morning/Night (*Asa [ni]*, *Yoru [ni]*).
> 3. **Forbidden:** Relative time words (e.g., *Kyou*, *Ashita*, *Kinou*, *Maiasa*). **Never** use "ni" with these!

## Concept 04: From and Until (〜から 〜まで)
* **から (kara)**: Starting point (From)
* **まで (made)**: Finishing point (Until)

These can be used for both **time** and **place**. They can be used together as a pair or individually.

* **9時から 5時まで 働（はたら）きます。**
    * *9-ji kara 5-ji made hatarakimasu.*
    * I work from 9 until 5.
* **銀行（ぎんこう）は 9時から 3時までです。**
    * *Ginkou wa 9-ji kara 3-ji made desu.*
    * The bank is [open] from 9 to 3.

---

## Concept 05: Connecting Nouns (と)
The particle **と (to)** is used to connect two nouns in a coordinate relation ("and").

* **銀行の休みは土曜日（どようび）と日曜日（にちようび）です。**
    * *Ginkou no yasumi wa doyoubi to nichiyoubi desu.*
    * The bank's holidays are Saturday **and** Sunday.

## Sidebar: Vocabulary & Cultural Nuance

### Time Vocabulary
| Kanji | Reading | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 今 | いま (Ima) | Now |
| 昨日 | きのう (Kinou) | Yesterday |
| 今日 | きょう (Kyou) | Today |
| 明日 | あした (Ashita) | Tomorrow |
| 毎朝 | まいあさ (Maiasa) | Every morning |
| 大変ですね | Taihen desu ne | That's tough / That's hard |

### Cultural Insight: The Suffix 〜ね (ne)
The particle **ね** is attached to the end of a sentence to seek agreement or show sympathy. It’s like saying "isn't it?" or "right?" in English.
* **A:** I study until 10 PM every day.
* **B:** **大変ですね。** (That's tough, isn't it?)

### Pro-Tip: Answering Verb Questions
When answering a question with a verb, **do not** use "Sou desu." You must repeat the verb in the correct tense.
* *Question:* Did you study?
* *Bad:* Hai, sou desu.
* *Good:* **はい、勉強しました。**
