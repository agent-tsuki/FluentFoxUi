## Concept 01: The Conditional 〜たら (tara)
The **〜たら** form is the most widely used conditional in Japanese. It is formed by adding **ら** to the **た-form** (Past Plain Form).

> **Pattern:** [V-た form] ら、[Main Sentence]。

### Meaning 1: "If" (Hypothetical)
* **お金（かね）が あったら、旅行（りょこう）します。**
    * *Okane ga attara, ryokou shimasu.*
    * **If** I have money, I will travel.

### Meaning 2: "When/After" (Certain Future)
* **10時（じゅうじ）に なったら、出（で）かけましょう。**
    * *Juuji ni nattara, dekakemashou.*
    * **When** it becomes 10 o'clock, let's head out.

---

## Concept 02: Reverse Conditionals (〜ても)
To express "even if" or "no matter how," use the **て-form** followed by **も**.

| Word Type | Connection | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Verb** | て-form + も | 調（しら）べ **ても** (Even if I look it up) |
| **い-adj** | 〜くても | 高（たか）**くても** (Even if it's expensive) |
| **な-adj / N** | 〜でも | 静（しず）か **でも** (Even if it's quiet) |

* **雨（あめ）が 降（ふ）っても、洗濯（せんたく）します。**
    * *Ame ga futte mo, sentaku shimasu.*
    * **Even if** it rains, I will do laundry.

---

## Concept 03: Giving Advice (〜たら いいですか)
Use this pattern when you are seeking advice or instructions on what to do in a specific situation.

* **パスポートを なくしたんですが、どうし**たら いいですか。**
    * *Pasupōto o nakushita n desu ga, doushittara ii desu ka?*
    * I've lost my passport; **what should I do?**
* **...警察（けいさつ）へ 行（い）ったら いいですよ。**
    * (...You should go to the police.)

---

## Concept 04: "As much as..." (Quantifier + も)
When **も** is added to a quantifier, it emphasizes that the amount is, in the speaker's opinion, quite a lot.

* **駅（えき）まで 2時間（にじかん）も かかりました。**
    * *Eki made ni-jikan mo kakarimashita.*
    * It took **as much as** two hours to get to the station.

---

## Concept 05: "Only" (Quantifier + しか)
As seen briefly before, **しか** is always followed by a **negative verb**. It implies that the amount is "not enough" or "only."

* **30分（さんじゅっぷん）しか 休みが ありません。**
    * *San-juppun shika yasumi ga arimasen.*
    * I **only** have 30 minutes of break.

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### 〜たら vs. 〜と
* **〜と (Ch. 21):** Used for automatic, natural results (If you push the button, the light turns on).
* **〜たら (Ch. 22):** Used for personal choices and one-time scenarios (If it's sunny, let's go to the park).


### Pro-Tip: The 'n desu' connection
In the example `nakushita n desu ga`, the **ん (n)** adds a nuance of explanation or "setting the scene" for the listener. It's very common when asking for help!
