## Concept 01: Asking for Permission (〜ても いいです)
To ask for or grant permission to do an action, use the **て-form** followed by **も いいです**.

* **ここで 写真（しゃしん）を 撮（と）っても いいですか。**
    * *Koko de shashin o totte mo ii desu ka.*
    * May I take a photo here?
    * **...はい、いいですよ。** (Yes, you may.)

---

## Concept 02: Expressing Prohibition (〜ては いけません)
To state that an action is prohibited (usually by law, rule, or common sense), use the **て-form** followed by **は いけません**.

* **ここで たばこを 吸（す）っては いけません。**
    * *Koko de tabako o sutte wa ikemasen.*
    * You must not smoke here.



---

## Concept 03: Resultant States (〜ています)
In Chapter 14, **〜ています** meant "Action in Progress." In this chapter, it refers to a **resultant state**—an action that happened in the past but the result still continues now.

### Common State Verbs:
* **知（し）っています**: I know (The state of having acquired knowledge).
* **住（す）んでいます**: I live in (The state of having moved in).
* **結婚（けっこん）しています**: I am married.
* **持（も）っています**: I have/own (The state of possessing).

> **Important Negation:** The negative of **知っています** is **知りません** (Not *shitte imasen*).

---

## Concept 04: Occupations and Habitual Actions
**〜ています** is also used to describe things you do regularly over a long period, such as your job or hobby.

* **IMCは コンピューターソフトを 作（つく）っています。**
    * *IMC wa konpyūtā sofuto o tsukutte imasu.*
    * IMC makes computer software (as a business).
* **わたしは 銀行（ぎんこう）で 働（はたら）いています。**
    * *Watashi wa ginkou de hataraite imasu.*
    * I work at a bank.

---

## Concept 05: Listing Actions with に (Place に N を V)
When several people are involved in an action, or when you are describing a location where a specific service is provided.

* **あそこの 売店（ばいてん）で お弁当（べんとう）を 売（う）っています。**
    * *Asoko no baiten de obentou o utte imasu.*
    * They sell bento at that stall over there.

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### Permission vs. Politeness
While `~te mo ii desu ka` is correct, if you are asking a very formal favor, you might hear `~te mo yoroshii desu ka`. For your learning portal, sticking to the standard form is best for N5 level.


### Comparison: Progressive vs. Resultant

* **Progressive:** I am currently eating. (Action is active)
* **Resultant:** I am married. (Action happened once, the state remains)
