## Concept 01: Modifying Nouns with Sentences
In previous chapters, we modified nouns with adjectives (*oishii mono*). Now, we use **Verbs in Plain Form** to describe nouns.

> **Rule:** The verb describing the noun must always be in **Plain Form** (Dictionary, Nai, Ta, or Nakatta form).

| English Structure | Japanese Structure | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| The person **who eats** | **食べる** 人 | *Taberu hito* |
| The person **who didn't eat** | **食べなかった** 人 | *Tabenakatta hito* |

---

## Concept 02: The Subject Particle が (ga) in Clauses
When a sentence modifies a noun, the subject of that small "sub-sentence" is marked with **が**, never **は**.

* **[ミラーさんが 作（つく）った] ケーキは おいしいです。**
    * *[Mirā-san ga tsukutta] kēki wa oishii desu.*
    * The cake [that Mr. Miller made] is delicious.
    * *(Note: Miller is the subject of the making, so we use 'ga')*

---

## Concept 03: Using Noun Phrases in Sentences
A noun modified by a sentence can function as the subject, object, or any other part of a larger sentence.

### As an Object:
* **[昨日（きのう） 買（か）った] 本（ほん）を 読（よ）みました。**
    * *[Kinou katta] hon o yomimashita.*
    * I read the book [that I bought yesterday].

### As a Subject:
* **[あそこに いる] 人（ひと）は 誰（だれ）ですか。**
    * *[Asoko ni iru] hito wa dare desu ka.*
    * Who is the person [who is over there]?

---

## Concept 04: Expressing Appointments & Tasks
You can use noun modification to talk about having "time to do something" or "an appointment to do something."

* **[病院（びょういん）へ 行（い）く] 時間（じかん）が ありません。**
    * *[Byouin e iku] jikan ga arimasen.*
    * I don't have **time [to go to the hospital]**.
* **[友達（ともadachi）と 会（あ）う] 約束（やくそく）が あります。**
    * *[Tomodachi to au] yakusoku ga arimasu.*
    * I have **an appointment [to meet a friend]**.

---

## Concept 05: Verb Phrases with あります
You can describe the existence of a specific state or situation using modified nouns.

* **今日（きょう）は [市役所（しやくしょ）へ 行く] 用事（ようじ）が あります。**
    * *Kyou wa [shiyakusho e iku] youji ga arimasu.*
    * Today I have **errands [to go to the city hall]**.

### Pro-Tip: Identifying the Main Verb
When sentences get long, look for the **last** verb/adjective—that is the main action. Everything before a noun is likely just a description of that noun.
* **[わたしが 昨日 撮った] 写真は [そこに] あります。**
* *The photo [that I took yesterday] is [over there].*
