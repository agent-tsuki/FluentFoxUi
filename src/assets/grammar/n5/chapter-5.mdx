## Concept 01: The Direction Particle (へ)
When you move toward a destination using verbs like **いきます** (go), **きます** (come), or **かえります** (return), the destination is marked with the particle **へ (e)**.

> **Note:** Although written as the Hiragana 'he', it is pronounced as **'e'** when used as a particle.

### Example Sentences
* **京都（きょうと）へ行（い）きます。**
    * *Kyōto e ikimasu.*
    * I am going to Kyoto.
* **日本（にほん）へ来（き）ました。**
    * *Nihon e kimashita.*
    * I came to Japan.
* **うちへ帰（かえ）ります。**
    * *Uchi e kaerimasu.*
    * I am going home.

---

## Concept 02: Total Negation (Interrogative + も)
To say you are going "nowhere" or doing "nothing," you attach the particle **も (mo)** to a question word (interrogative) followed by a **negative verb**.

* **どこ [へ] も行きません。**
    * *Doko [e] mo ikimasen.*
    * I’m not going anywhere.
* **何も食べません。**
    * *Nani mo tabemasen.*
    * I’m not going to eat anything.

---

## Concept 03: Means of Transport (〜で 行きます)
The particle **で (de)** indicates the means, method, or tool used to perform an action. In this context, it marks your vehicle.

### Vehicles & Walking
* **電車（でんしゃ）で行きます。** (I’m going by train.)
* **タクシーで来ました。** (I came by taxi.)

> **Exception:** If you are walking, do **not** use the particle "de." Use the specific expression **歩（ある）いて (aruite)**.
> * **駅（えき）から歩いて帰りました。** (I walked home from the station.)

---

## Concept 04: Traveling with People (〜と / ひとりで)
* **[Person/Animal] と**: Indicates "with" someone.
    * **家族（かぞく）と日本へ来ました。** (I came to Japan with my family.)
* **ひとりで**: If you are doing something alone, use this expression. No particle "to" is needed.
    * **ひとりで東京（とうきょう）へ行きます。** (I am going to Tokyo by myself.)

---

## Concept 05: Asking "When?" (いつ)
**いつ (Itsu)** is the general word for "when." Unlike specific time words (like 9-ji), **いつ does not take the particle に**.

* **いつ日本へ来ましたか。**
    * *Itsu Nihon e kimashita ka.*
    * When did you come to Japan?
    * **...3月25日に来ました。** (...I came on March 25th.)

---

## Sidebar: Conversational Endings

### Assertive Information (〜よ)
The particle **よ (yo)** is placed at the end of a sentence to emphasize information the listener doesn't know, or to express a strong opinion.
* **この電車は甲子園（こうしえん）へ行きますか。**
* **...いいえ、行きません。次の「普通（ふつう）」ですよ。** (No, it doesn't. You need the next local train, you know.)

### Agreement & Sympathy (〜ね)
As seen before, **ね (ne)** is for seeking agreement. 
* **あしたは日曜日ですね。** (Tomorrow is Sunday, right?)
* **...あ、そうですね。** (Oh, yes, it is.)
