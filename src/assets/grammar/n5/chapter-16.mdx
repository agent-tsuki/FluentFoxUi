## Concept 01: Conjugating the ない-form (Nai-form)
The conjugation rules for the negative plain form (Nai-form) follow the same group logic as the Te-form.

### Group I Conjugation Rules:
Change the **~i** sound before ~masu to the corresponding **~a** sound and add **ない**.

| ~masu ending | Changes to... | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **い** | 〜わ (wa) | 会います → **会わない** |
| **き** | 〜か (ka) | 書きます → **書かない** |
| **ぎ** | 〜が (ga) | 急ぎます → **急がない** |
| **し** | 〜さ (sa) | 話します → **話さない** |
| **ち** | 〜た (ta) | 待ちます → **待たない** |
| **に** | 〜な (na) | 死にます → **死なない** |
| **び** | 〜ば (ba) | 遊びます → **遊ばない** |
| **み** | 〜ま (ma) | 読みます → **読まない** |
| **り** | 〜ら (ra) | 取ります → **取らない** |

### Group II & III:
* **Group II:** Just replace **ます** with **ない** (e.g., 食べます → **食べない**).
* **Group III:** します → **しない** / きます → **こない** (Note the pronunciation change!).
* **Special Case:** The verb **あります** (to have/exist) becomes simply **ない**.

## Concept 02: Polite Negative Requests (〜ないで ください)
Use this pattern to ask someone **not** to do something.

* **ここで 写真（しゃしん）を 撮（と）らないで ください。**
    * *Koko de shashin o toranaide kudasai.*
    * Please don't take photos here.
* **危（あぶ）ないですから、入（はい）らないで ください。**
    * *Abunai desu kara, hairanaide kudasai.*
    * It's dangerous, so please don't enter.

---

## Concept 03: Expressing Obligation (〜なければ なりません)
This pattern expresses "must" or "have to." Despite being a double negative in structure, it functions as a strong affirmative obligation.

* **薬（くすり）を 飲（の）まなければ なりません。**
    * *Kusuri o nomanakereba narimasen.*
    * I must take my medicine.
* **毎日（まいにち） 漢字（かんじ）を 勉強（べんきょう）しなければ なりません。**
    * *Mainichi Kanji o benkyou shinakereba narimasen.*
    * I have to study Kanji every day.

---

## Concept 04: Expressing Lack of Necessity (〜なくても いいです)
This is the opposite of obligation: "don't have to" or "don't need to."

* **明日（あした） 来（こ）なくても いいです。**
    * *Ashita konakete mo ii desu.*
    * You don't have to come tomorrow.
* **急（いそ）がなくても いいです。**
    * *Isoganakute mo ii desu.*
    * You don't need to hurry.

---

## Concept 05: Object as Topic (N は)
When you want to emphasize the object of a sentence (usually in negative sentences or for contrast), you can replace the particle **を** with **は**.

* **ここに 荷物（にもつ）は 置（お）かないで ください。**
    * *Koko ni nimotsu wa okanaide kudasai.*
    * (As for the luggage) please do not put it here.

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### Pronunciation Check: きます (Group III)
The verb "to come" is famously tricky:
* Dictionary: くる (kuru)
* Polite: きます (kimasu)
* Negative: **こない (konai)** — The kanji stays the same (来), but the reading changes.


### Cultural Insight: The Length of Obligations
The phrase `~nakereba narimasen` is quite a mouthful for beginners. In casual speech, Japanese people often shorten this to `~nakya` or `~nakucha`, but for your N5 studies and the government job portal's professional context, the full polite form is the one to master.
