## Concept 01: Two Types of Adjectives
Japanese adjectives are divided into two groups based on how they conjugate and connect to nouns.

1.  **な-adjectives (na-adj):** Act like nouns in many ways. They need a **な** to connect to a noun.
2.  **い-adjectives (i-adj):** Always end in a dictionary-form **い**. They connect directly to nouns.

---

## Concept 02: Adjectives as Predicates
When an adjective comes at the end of a sentence to describe a subject ($N \text{ は Adjective です}$), they show politeness toward the listener.

### Non-Past Negative Conjugations
| Type | Affirmative (Yes) | Negative (No) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **な-adj** | 〜です | 〜じゃ（では）ありません |
| **い-adj** | 〜い です | 〜く ない です |

> **Crucial Exception:** The negative of **いい** (Good) is **よく ない です**.

### Examples:
* **あそこは 静（しず）かじゃありません。** (It is not quiet there.)
* **この本（ほん）はおもしろくないです。** (This book is not interesting.)

---

## Concept 03: Modifying Nouns
To describe a noun directly (e.g., "a kind teacher"), the placement is: **[Adjective] + [Noun]**.

* **な-adj:** Keep the **な**.
    * **親切（しんせつ）な 先生（せんせい）** (A kind teacher)
* **い-adj:** Use the dictionary form.
    * **高（たか）い 山（やま）** (A high mountain)

---

## Concept 04: Degree Adverbs (とても & あまり)
These words modify the "strength" of the adjective and always come before it.

* **とても (Totemo):** "Very." Used in affirmative sentences.
    * **ペキンは とても 寒（さむ）いです。** (Beijing is very cold.)
* **あまり (Amari):** "Not very." Used **only** with negative sentences.
    * **シャンハイは あまり 寒くないです。** (Shanghai is not very cold.)

---

## Concept 05: Connecting Ideas with 〜が
The particle **が (ga)** acts as "but" to connect two contrasting statements.

* **日本の食（た）べ物（もの）はおいしいですが、高（たか）いです。**
    * *Nihon no tabemono wa oishii desu ga, takai desu.*
    * Japanese food is delicious, **but** it is expensive.

---

## Concept 06: Asking for Opinions
Use these interrogatives to inquire about the listener's impressions or nature of a thing.

1.  **N は どうですか:** "How is N?" (Asking for an impression).
    * **日本の生活（せいかつ）はどうですか。** (How do you find life in Japan?)
2.  **どんな N:** "What kind of N?".
    * **奈良（なら）は どんな 町（まち）ですか。** (What sort of town is Nara?)

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### Conversation Filler: そうですね
While in Chapter 5 `そうですね` was used for agreement, here it is used to show you are **thinking** about an answer.
* **A:** How is your work?
* **B:** **そうですね。忙（いそが）しいですが、おもしろいです。** (Well... it's busy, but interesting.)
