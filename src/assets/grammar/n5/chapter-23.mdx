## Concept 01: Giving & Receiving Objects
Japanese distinguishes between giving to others and someone giving to "me" (the speaker).

| Verb | Meaning | Direction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **あげます** (agemasu) | To give | Speaker → Others / Someone → Others |
| **もらいます** (moraimasu) | To receive | Speaker ← Others |
| **くれます** (kuremasu) | To give (to me) | Others → Speaker (or my family) |

### Key Patterns:
* **[Giver] は [Receiver] に [Object] を あげます。**
* **[Receiver] は [Giver] に/から [Object] を もらいます。**
* **[Giver] は [わたし] に [Object] を くれます。**

---

## Concept 02: Giving & Receiving Favors (Te-form + Verb)
When you do something for someone, or someone does something for you, you use the **て-form** of the action verb followed by the giving/receiving verbs.

### 1. 〜て あげます (Doing a favor)
* **わたしは 木村（きむら）さんに 本（ほん）を 貸（か）して あげました。**
    * *Watashi wa Kimura-san ni hon o kashite agemashita.*
    * I lent a book to Ms. Kimura (as a favor).

### 2. 〜て もらいます (Receiving a favor)
* **わたしは 鈴木（すずき）さんに 日本語（にほんご）を 教（おし）えて もらいました。**
    * *Watashi wa Suzuki-san ni Nihongo o oshiete moraimashita.*
    * I had Mr. Suzuki teach me Japanese (I received the favor of teaching).

### 3. 〜て くれます (Someone doing something for me)
* **佐藤（さとう）さんは 傘（かさ）を 貸（か）して くれました。**
    * *Satou-san wa kasa o kashite kuremashita.*
    * Mr. Sato lent me an umbrella (He kindly did it for me).

---

## Concept 03: The Subject Particle が (ga) with Kuremasu
When using **〜て くれます**, the person who performed the favor is the subject and is often emphasized with **が**.

* **誰（だれ）が 手伝（てつだ）って くれましたか。**
    * *Dare ga tetsudatte kuremashita ka?*
    * Who helped you?
    * **...山田（やまだ）さんが 手伝って くれました。** (...Mr. Yamada helped me.)

---

## Concept 04: Interrogatives with Giving/Receiving
When you want to ask who gave what to whom:

* **誰（だれ）に その時計（とけい）を もらいましたか。**
    * *Dare ni sono tokei o moraimashita ka?*
    * Who did you receive that watch from?

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### Important Distinction: Ageru vs. Kuremasu
* Use **あげます** when you are the giver or observing one person giving to another.
* **NEVER** use **あげます** when someone gives to **you**. You must use **くれます**.


### Pro-Tip: Social Hierarchy
In your portal project, if you were to implement a "Peer Review" or "Mentorship" feature, these verbs would be essential. A mentor **教（おし）えて くれます** (teaches me), while a student **教（おし）えて もらいます** (receives the teaching).
