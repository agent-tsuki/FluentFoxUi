## Concept 01: Polite Form vs. Plain Form
Japanese has two main styles of speech. Up until now, you have focused on **Polite Form (丁寧形 - Teinei-kei)**.

* **Polite Form:** Used with strangers, superiors, or in formal settings. Ends in **〜ます** or **〜です**.
* **Plain Form:** Used with close friends, family, or people younger/lower in rank. Also used in written materials like newspapers and books.

---

## Concept 02: Conjugating to Plain Form
To speak casually, every part of the sentence (Verbs, Adjectives, and Nouns) must switch to its plain equivalent.

### 1. Verbs
| Polite Form | Plain Form | Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 書きます (Affirmative) | **書く** | Dictionary Form |
| 書きません (Negative) | **書かない** | Nai-form |
| 書きました (Past Aff.) | **書いた** | Ta-form |
| 書きませんでした (Past Neg.) | **書かなかった** | Nakatta-form |

### 2. い-Adjectives
Simply drop the **です**.
* 安い です → **安い**
* 安くない です → **安くない**

### 3. な-Adjectives & Nouns
Replace **です** with **だ**.
| Form | Polite | Plain |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Affirmative | 雨 です | **雨 だ** |
| Negative | 雨 じゃありません | **雨 じゃな­い** |
| Past Aff. | 雨 でした | **雨 だった** |
| Past Neg. | 雨 じゃありませんでした | **雨 じゃなかった** |

---

## Concept 03: Casual Interrogatives (Questions)
In casual speech, the question particle **か** is usually dropped. Instead, the speaker uses a **rising intonation**.

* **Polite:** コーヒーを 飲みます**か**。
* **Plain:** コーヒー [を] 飲む？ (Rising tone)

> **Note:** In casual speech, particles like **を, は, へ** are often omitted if the meaning is clear.

---

## Concept 04: Plain Form + と 思います (I think...)
Use this to express an opinion or a guess. The clause before **と** must always be in **Plain Form**.

* **明日（あした）は 雨（あめ）が 降（ふ）ると 思（おも）います。**
    * *Ashita wa ame ga furu to omoimasu.*
    * I think it will rain tomorrow.
* **日本（にほん）は 物価（ぶっか）が 高（たか）いと 思います。**
    * *Nihon wa bukka ga takai to omoimasu.*
    * I think prices in Japan are high.

---

## Concept 05: Plain Form + と 言いました (He/She said...)
To report what someone else said, use the **Plain Form** clause followed by **と言いました**.

* **ミラーさんは 「明日（あした） 休（やす）みます」と言（い）いました。** (Direct Quote)
* **ミラーさんは 明日 休むと 言いました。** (Indirect Quote)
    * *Mirā-san wa ashita yasumu to iimashita.*
    * Mr. Miller said he will take a day off tomorrow.

---

## Sidebar: Reference & Nuance

### Conversation Filler: でしょう？
**でしょう** is used when you expect the listener to agree with you (like "right?"). In casual speech, it becomes **だろ**.
* **北海道（ほっかいどう）は 寒（さむ）かったでしょう？** (Hokkaido was cold, wasn't it?)


### Cultural Insight: Male vs. Female Casual Speech

In casual conversation, sentence-ending particles often differ. Men might use **だ** or **だぞ**, while women might omit **だ** or use **ね**. However, for your N5 exam and professional portal, understanding the neutral plain form is the priority!
